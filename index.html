Self-consistent MHD simulations of Interstellar Medium/Star Fomration/Feedback

TIGRESS is a novel framework for multi-physics numerical simulations of the star-forming interstellar medium (ISM) implemented in the Athena MHD code.

Star formation and the properties of the interstellar medium (ISM) are tightly coupled because stars are born in the ISM, which maintains turbulence and multiple thermal phases by gaining energy and momentum from stellar feedback. However, it has been a common practice to model one process in detail, while the other is held fixed. This approach disconnects the connected processes in a system, limiting the predictive power of such models. The development of a novel numerical framework, TIGRESS, in which star formation, stellar feedback, and ISM physics are modeled self-consistently, facilitates quantitative predictions to essential properties of the star-forming ISM, such as turbulence characteristics, thermal phase balance, star formation rates, and multiphase outflow properties.

SFR self-regulation

Local galactic conditions (weight of gas) demands a certain level of star formation rates to provide support by feedback. SFRs are self-regulated to meet the demand.

Winds and Fountains

SN feedback drives hot galactic winds and warm fountains.

MHD dynamo

SN driven turbulence and galactic differential rotation enable galactic dyanmo action.

Dust polarization maps

Self-consistent MHD turbulence in the local ISM produces polarized dust emission consistent with Planck observations.

XCO

CO-to-H2 conversion factor (X_CO) is investigaetd in the TIGRESS suite. CO is only an approximate tracer of molecular gas.

Ram pressure stripping

The realistic interstellar medium in TIGRESS interacts with the intracluster medium.

Metal Mixing

Ly-alpha radiation transfer

UV radiation feedback
